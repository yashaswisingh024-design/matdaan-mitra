Step by Step

How Elections Work in India

Follow these key steps to participate in the world's largest democracy.

01
📝

Voter Registration

Every Indian citizen aged 18+ can register to vote. Apply online at voters.eci.gov.in using Form 6 or visit your local BLO (Booth Level Officer).

  • ✔ Aadhaar Card / Proof of Identity
  • ✔ Proof of Address
  • ✔ Passport-size Photograph
  • ✔ Age Proof (if under 21)
Start Here
02
📋

Electoral Roll (Voter List)

The Electoral Roll is the official list of all registered voters in a constituency. The Election Commission of India (ECI) publishes and updates this list regularly.

  • ✔ Search your name at voters.eci.gov.in
  • ✔ Roll is updated before every election
  • ✔ Corrections can be requested via Form 8
  • ✔ EPIC (Voter ID Card) is issued after enrollment
Check Status
03
🏫

Polling Booth

On Election Day, voters go to their designated Polling Booth — usually a school, community hall, or government building. Each booth serves a specific geographic area.

  • ✔ Carry your Voter ID or alternate ID
  • ✔ Check booth location on the ECI app
  • ✔ Booths open 7 AM – 6 PM (varies by state)
  • ✔ Ink mark on finger confirms voting
Election Day
04
🖲️

EVM Voting

India uses Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs) for secure, tamper-proof elections. The VVPAT machine prints a slip so you can verify your vote was recorded correctly.

  • ✔ Press the button next to your candidate
  • ✔ A beep confirms your vote
  • ✔ VVPAT shows a 7-second paper slip
  • ✔ One voter, one vote — fully encrypted
Cast Your Vote
05
📊

Result & Counting

After voting ends, EVMs are sealed and stored securely. On counting day, votes are tallied constituency-wise. Results are declared by the Returning Officer.

  • ✔ Counting usually happens a few days later
  • ✔ Party/alliance with majority forms the government
  • ✔ Results published on ECI website
  • ✔ Candidates can request a recount
See Results
06
⚖️

Model Code of Conduct

The Model Code of Conduct (MCC) is a set of guidelines issued by the ECI that governs political parties and candidates during the election period.

  • ✔ No hate speech or communal appeals
  • ✔ No misuse of government resources
  • ✔ No announcement of new schemes after MCC
  • ✔ Violations can lead to disqualification

NOTA (None Of The Above) is an option on the EVM that allows voters to reject all candidates. It was introduced in 2013 by a Supreme Court ruling. Votes for NOTA are counted but do not affect the election result directly.

EVMs are standalone machines — not connected to any network. Votes are stored in encrypted memory chips. The machines are sealed after polling, and only vote totals (not individual votes) are revealed during counting, ensuring complete ballot secrecy.

The Election Commission of India (ECI) is an autonomous constitutional authority responsible for administering election processes in India. It was established on 25 January 1950 and oversees Lok Sabha, Rajya Sabha, and State Legislative Assembly elections.

The minimum age to vote in India is 18 years. The voter's age is calculated as of 1st January of the year the electoral roll is prepared. Youth aged 17 can pre-register and will automatically be enrolled when they turn 18.
